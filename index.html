So you're probably wondering... why?

The answer I have for you is simply infinite.

Well, what could possibly be infinite, mate?

Imagination.

Writing code is like a painting. Both require the utilisation of imagination for a deeper understanding. When creating the painting, the artist must consider the viewer's imagination for interpretation. In contrast to the web developer, the interpretation comes from the creator's imagination.

Perhaps some elaboration?

Art.

For me, I've always admired art. Painting on a blank canvas was illustrating my emotions into something physical, something visual. However, my emotions and ideas were always cut short by the canvas's margins. Desperately, I searched for months looking for a solution and here it is:


<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
    <head>...</head>
    <body>...</body>
</html>

These simple lines of HTML code was the blank canvas I was searching for. In five lines, I started my journey in painting whatever the hell I wanted. The canvas was an infinitely blank sheet and was only bounded by my imagination: a bound set at infinity.

Where are these canvasses you speak of?

Here.
